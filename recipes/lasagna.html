Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of pasta, rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and melted mozzarella. Baked until golden and bubbling, it is a hearty, comforting meal often enjoyed at family gatherings or special occasions. Each layer adds flavor and texture, making lasagna a beloved favorite around the world.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles (about 12 sheets)
  • 1 lb ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara or pasta sauce
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil (for cooking)
  • Optional: chopped basil or parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a pan, heat olive oil and cook the ground beef until browned. Drain excess fat.
  3. Add marinara sauce to the meat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with the egg, salt, and pepper.
  5. Boil lasagna noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  6. In a baking dish, spread a layer of meat sauce, then add noodles, ricotta mixture, and mozzarella. Repeat layers untill all ingredients are used.
  7. Top with remaining sauce, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheese.
  8. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  9. Remove foil and bake uncovered for another 15 minutes, or until cheese is golden and bubbly.
  10. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
