Chocolate Chip Cookies

Home

Description

Chocolate chip cookies are a timeless treat known for their golden-brown edges, chewy centers, and bursts of rich chocolate in every bite. Perfect with a glass of milk or a cup of coffee, these cookies are easy to make and loved by kids and adults alike. Whether fresh out of the oven or stored in a cookie jar, they’re a classic comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • Optional: 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then add the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  5. Gradually mix the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients until fully combined.
  6. Stir in the chocolate chips (and nuts, if using).
  7. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet, leaving space between each.
  8. Bake for 9–11 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 2 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
  10. Enjoy warm or store in an airtight container for up to a week.
